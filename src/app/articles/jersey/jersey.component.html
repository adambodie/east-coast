Driving through New Jersey, the Joy of My Life

{{date}}

It’s true what they say, New Jersey is the joke state. No one takes New Jersey seriously, and most people who live there are those who can’t afford to live in New York or Philadelphia. Driving through New Jersey was incredibly boring and lame. It’s bad enough that they have bad and impatient drivers, but the New Jersey Turnpike, the road we drove on, was hardly scenic by any means. Just a boring highway with trees side by side and no cities around.

Sure, there were a few minor good things about the New Jersey Turnpike. Their rest areas are frequent and modern, with a few shops and gas station at each one. Nothing like the rest stops they have here in Oregon. I remember on rest stop near Hermiston so disgusting, it smelled like shit and I about wanted to puke. Another plus for the New Jersey Turnpike is that they have an elevated road just for the big trucks. With New Jersey drivers as bad as they are, getting the trucks out of the way and isolated is a great thing, I doubt the 100mph New Jersey drivers would have any patience with a truck going the speed limit.

Finally, it gets interesting

{{date}}

Eventually, New Jersey started to have some great views. Not surprisingly, they're views of New York City. My first impression of New York City was one of amazement. It almost looked surreal, seeing nothing but tall skyscrapers lined up in row, even though we were like 10 miles away. The tallest building in the country, the One World Trade Center, was incredibly huge and phenomenal, even from afar. The Empire State Building, from afar, looked every bit as beautiful as described throughout the world.

The closer we got, the bigger and more amazing the buildings looked, even the tall skyscraper in Jersey City looked nice, but only with the far bigger New York Skyline. I could hardly contain myself as we made our way to New York City. I just wanted in. But with Jenn driving, we needed to make one more stop at a gas station, with a bathroom break needed as well. Of course, New Jersey is the only other state besides Oregon that doesn't allow you to fill up your own gas, so this was the one place Jenn could feel comfortable pulling into the gas station, as she drove through most of New Jersey. Plus Jenn could never drive in New York City, so Neal took over at that point. When it was all said and done, a ride into the Holland Tunnel awaited, with New York City, at last, on the other side.
