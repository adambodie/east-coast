You Can Call Me Al

Back in the day, You Can Call Me Al was a song performed by Paul Simon, made famous with a video containing Chevy Chase lip-synching the lyrics. Of course, anyone heading to the D.C.-Baltimore region should know that Chevy Chase is also the name of a Washington D.C. suburb. So it was only fitting that on the way to the airport, this song got brought up in the car and stuck in my head. So what exactly is this song about, the very song I played drums when I was just 2 years old?

Extensive Research led me to find this out: The song, written by Paul Simon, appears to be about a man in a midlife crisis. Simon was inspired to write this song after making a trip to South Africa, which led him to make the Graceland album which this song is featured as a single. As for the names in the some, Al and Betty, Paul Simon came up with those names from a party which him and his wife Peggy were mistaken for Al and Betty.

Dinner Review: Pho Kevin

How to Eat Pho by Neal Pitts

Our first night in Maryland led us to try Vietnamese food, particularly their Pho, Vietnamese noodle soup that's considered a specialty. But making Pho, like a bicycle, requires multiple steps to achieve its tastiness and unique flavors. With that said, here were the instructions Neal gave us for preparing our Pho.

  1. Give Adam all the White Onions. If you don't like onions, don't order them in your Pho. Otherwise, I’d be glad to add their rich, crisp flavor into my Pho.
  2. Sprinkle two leaves of cilantro into small pieces into the Pho.
  3. Squeeze Lime Juice into the Pho.
  4. Add a chunk of sprouts on top for texture.
  5. Add a dash of plum sauce and/or sriracha for flavor and spice. Plum sauce isn't spicy, but Sriracha clearly is.
  6. Drop jalapeños if you like it super hot. Warning, don't bite into it unless you want your tongue to burn.
  7. Scoop with spoon, using chopsticks to hold noodles closely with the broth in spoon.
  8. Move your mouth close to the spoon and enjoy.
  9. Repeat steps seven and eight until your meal is done.

Also on the menu was a Thai Iced Tea, which serves as a delicious, sweet and tasty drink made from tea, milk and sugar. Added for extra tastiness were the Boba, also known as the Tapioca balls. Another nice way to begin our adventurous trip.
