Statue of Liberty by the Numbers

1886 - The year the Statue of Liberty was dedicated, a gift from the people of France, designed by Frederic Auguste Bartholdi and built by Gustave Eiffel, the latter who built the Eiffel Tower.

2 - Thickness in stacked pennies used in of the copper used for the Statue of Liberty

14 - Approximate number of years after completion that the Statue of Liberty began to turn into its greenish color. Originally a dull copper color, it changed due to an oxidation process of the copper known as verdigris. It remained that color due to public protest, though the inside was painted.

1 - The number of successful suicides to occur at the Statue of Liberty, which occurred in 1929

1956 - The year Liberty Island got its name, changed from its original name, Bedloe Island

Heading back to Manhattan was a breeze, as it was easier to get on the ferry, although there was less sitting room. Hungry for lunch, we had hot dogs and chips to fill us up. Nothing too fancy, but satisfying at the very least. But as there are multiple islands in the New York Harbor, one additional stop was necessary before heading back to Manhattan: Ellis Island.

Though we didn't step onto Ellis Island, as it was not part of our agenda, I still thought it was neat to see from the ferry. The building itself looked nice, and views from the distance also provided a nice backdrop. It's amazing to see a place where many people entered the United States the first time. With my ancestors in Europe, it's a reminder that we all come from somewhere, and perhaps some of my ancestors came to the United States at Ellis Island. I believe my ancestors entered the United States via Boston, but I can imagine what it was like to enter the United States via boat and first see the Statue of Liberty. After stopping at Ellis Island, the visit to the Statue of Liberty was officially over. It was time to get back to Manhattan and begin the mad dash back to our hotel, which proved to be more interesting than one could ever expect.
