The Most Unexpected Ride to D.C.

{{date}}

The next morning was not rushed as Neal had to take his car in and complete some job related stuff at home. Jenn and I slept in a little and got ready for our adventure in Washington D.C. Without a car, that meant Jenn and I go off on our own and meet Neal later. The plan: Uber it to the Greenbelt Metro and hop on the Metro from there. But life throws you curveballs. Instead of going to what appeared to be a closer College Park Metro stop, we ended up at the Greenbelt stop, only for the Uber driver to be confused as to where to drop us off. With the Uber drivers lack of understanding and our inexperience with the Metro, the Uber driver made it up to us by driving us straight to D.C., all while not charging us for the additional miles driven. Who knew this would happen all because they use a ridiculously archaic term “Kiss and Ride” for drop offs? Do people actually kiss their loved ones before heading off? The big mystery in all of this. Once we arrived in the nation’s Capital, we were dropped off at the U.S. Capitol, but first, it was time to eat.

The Alibi

With neither of us certain of where to eat, and not much options readily visible, we found the nearest place on the map, a British Pub called the Alibi, which had the feel of a British Pub, but unfortunately, didn't have very good service, as they were too slow, especially for “cranky from not eating anything” Jenn. I unfortunately got the brunt of the crankiness. As for the food, it was decent pub food. I had a sausage sandwich with fries. But Jenn’s crankiness set a bad mood and I simply wanted to get out of there A.S.A.P. due to it. Plus I wanted to visit D.C., since we were suddenly on a time crunch to see Galvin.

Capitol Mall: The Hellhole Edition

{{date}}

After lunch, we made our way back to visit the workplace of all but one of the most worthless bunch of Politicians ever assembled; the Republicans who can’t accomplish anything and want to give my money away to the wealthy. The U.S. Capitol is a pretty building, an example of state capitols, and the Reflection pool gave it a more pleasant view. I only visited the Capitol once before when I first went to D.C. In 2005, but didn't see much of it as I was young and had limited camera space. Times have improved much more since then.

Also on the Agenda was the Capitol Mall, an area I only briefly visited in 2005 as well. Of course, Jenn didn’t want to walk, so I gave her a bench to sit on while I did all the exploring. A large grassy area full of the Smithsonian Art Museums and the Washington Monument in the distance provided a perfect backdrop for the aesthetic qualities of D.C. It was also during this walk that the weather started to lose its sun, as dark clouds slowly started to come in. Jenn wanted me to return as apparently she was more hot and sweaty by sitting than walking. She also heard a storm was coming, as did I in a not to far location. Having reached the Smithsonian Castle, I knew it was time to come back. A lot of artwork in this area, which I really enjoyed. But with limited time, I didn't get the full experience. Having been the D.C. Before, it wasn’t that big of a deal.

For the Exercise Buffs

{{date}}

Walking through the National Mall on a hot and muggy day was challenging at times. Of course, I should note I didn't start my watch until I reached the middle of the National Gallery of Art. I started walking at the edge of the Mall, where I finished up. I spent plenty of time zig-zagging, looking for better pictures to take. I realized it was better to be closer to get pictures of the Museums. Once I reached the Smithsonian, I moved back to get a better view.

Though I contemplated going further, Jenn, of course, texted me wanting me to come back. I knew I needed to get back to Jenn. Even more incentive to return was a storm on its way. I was not prepared to walk in what ended up being a nasty storm. So I rushed back the best I could, stopping at the Sculpture Garden for a few nice pictures. After that, it was a straight shot back to Jenn.

Clearly, I burned a lot of calories during the recorded portion of this half hour walk, having covered over a mile of walking in what looks like a just a few blocks. Clearly my heart was pumping, which shows based on the analysis of my heart rate. In all, a productive exercise. Thankfully, Jenn held on to my backpack.
