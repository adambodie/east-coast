Gonna Fly Now: The True Story of the Rocky Experience and More

The Philadelphia Museum of Art is one of the more famous Art Museums in the U.S., though modern fame has little to do with the Art itself. Aside from providing great city views with a classical architecture that would make the Ancient Romans proud, the Museum was made famous by Rocky, the seminal 1976 coming of age boxing film starring Sylvester Stallone as the lovable underdog Rocky Balboa, who ran up the steps of the Museum in what’s colloquially known as the Rocky Steps. The ground level was beautiful and provided a lovely scene. Also there was the Rocky Statue that plenty of people were drawn to.

Neal dragged Jenn up the 72 steps, but I ran up them despite the late start and my backpack, beating the lazy tag team. I meant to record the video from a first person view, but sadly, I didn't press the record button until after I reached the top. I didn’t feel it was necessary to do it again. Though America’s most cliche place to get exercise, the area on the whole was quite beautiful, with a great view of the city. It reminded me of the St. Louis Art Museum, the architecture was similar, but with better surrounding views and golden architecture, the Art Museum is a great place to visit. Just watch out for the bums. Gotta love tourist traps.

Philadelphia Skyline

{{date}}

Philadelphia's Skyline continues to flourish, becoming one of the tallest outside the two mega-skylines, Chicago and New York. Current plans for the new Comcast Technology Center, currently under construction, will become the tallest building in the United States outside of Chicago and New York when completed, expected to be next year. The Comcast Center, currently its tallest building, was completed in 2008.

For years, Philadelphia had a unofficial agreement to not build anything taller than Philadelphia City Hall, at 548 feet tall. Since the One Liberty Place was built in 1987, ending that arrangement, Philadelphia has seen multiple skyscrapers rise up the charts, and even more are in the works to join that list.
