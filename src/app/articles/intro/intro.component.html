The flight to Baltimore was mostly easy. Thanks to advances in phone photography, pictures taken from a plane can be located thanks to GPS. As such, I can make an approximate path taken. Not surprisingly, there's a large chunk of time spent in big and wide Montana. We did fly above the town of Bozeman, among other places. Our first night in Maryland led us to try Vietnamese food, particularly their Pho, Vietnamese noodle soup that's considered a specialty. But making Pho, like a bicycle, requires multiple steps to achieve its tastiness and unique flavors. With that said, here were the instructions Neal gave us for preparing our Pho.

  1. Give Adam all the White Onions. If you don't like onions, don't order them in your Pho. Otherwise, I’d be glad to add their rich, crisp flavor into my Pho.
  2. Sprinkle two leaves of cilantro into small pieces into the Pho.
  3. Squeeze Lime Juice into the Pho.
  4. Add a chunk of sprouts on top for texture.
  5. Add a dash of plum sauce and/or sriracha for flavor and spice. Plum sauce isn't spicy, but Sriracha clearly is.
  6. Drop jalapeños if you like it super hot. Warning, don't bite into it unless you want your tongue to burn.
  7. Scoop with spoon, using chopsticks to hold noodles closely with the broth in spoon.
  8. Move your mouth close to the spoon and enjoy.
  9. Repeat steps seven and eight until your meal is done.

As for the restaurant itself, it had the feel of a typical subarban restaurant; nothing too special or fancy. Jenn wanted to try the Coconut Water, but of course, they were out of it. She would have loved the Thai Iced Tea, but with Caffeine, she can't have it. She tried a sip of my Thai Iced Tea, which I hadn't had in years. Jenn regretted it, since it was so tasty. I tried the Boba within the drink, also known as the Tapioca balls. I had never tried it before, but they were tasty and made the drink better. Another nice way to begin our adventurous trip.
