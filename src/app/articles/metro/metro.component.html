Good news for Jenn and Neal, the next Metro was three minutes behind.

After both of us reached the Greenbelt Metro station, one more Uber ride awaited us to take us to the Honda and pick up Neal’s car. Driving there provided something I wouldn’t expect in the area, a country like landscape. No city life, no suburban life. Certainly that can be expected in Portland, but not in the D.C. Metro. Dinner and a Show at Sakura

{{date}}

With one more night in Maryland, we had one more friend of Neal's to visit, Kelli. The only one of Neal's friends I had met on the previous trip, we met up at our hotel. Previously, Neal asked Jenny if she wanted to come as well. Of course, Siri had other plans as it asked her if she wanted to come to Laurel for denture center. Neal tried a little too hard to enunciate dinner, opting for the more aspirate sounding dinter. You gotta love Siri lol.

That led us to Sakura, a Japanese Steak Seafood & Sushi Bar, which saw us at a Benihana styled restaurant, only with better guests. It was mostly our crew, with a young couple already there for the festivities. Jenny and her daughter Payton made it not too long after, though they weren't there to eat. I ordered a Love Potion #9, a Vodka based drink with Schnapps and Grenadine. Certainly a love potion indeed. Once our food got made, the fireworks began with the flames, eggs in the hat routine, the choo choo onion tower, and more. An evolution of meals was ready to begin, starting with Chicken Livers, culminating with the Steak and Shrimp combo with rice, veggies and yummy dipping sauce.

After dinner was done, and Neal, not surprisingly, being the center of attention, it was time to wrap up and head back to our places. But first, we needed dessert before our departure to New York City. So we frantically drove to a frozen yogurt place, Sweet Frog, with just minutes left on the clock before it closed at 10:00. Like a stampeding herd of buffalo, we charged into the shop and got ourselves some yummy frozen yogurt. I got some Chocolate & Peanut Butter and Creme Brulee. How could I say no to Creme Brulee? One last stop was needed to go to Target, since Kelli needed something. Not wasting the opportunity, we got some snacks and waters for the New York trip. Jenny helped save a rather ugly looking bug from certain Automatic Door Doom. And just like that, it was time to embark on the biggest trip of our lives...New York City
